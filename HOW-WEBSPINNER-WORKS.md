# How Webspinner Works

*A patron's-eye walkthrough of the site you are visiting and the loom that wove it.*

## One Sentence Becomes a Website

iPhloat began as a sentence: *a waterproof iPhone case that floats face-up.*

From that sentence — and the imagery and prose its owner authored to give it substance — every page on iphloat.com was woven. There is no template here. No copy that was once handed to a designer and then frozen. The page you are reading, the chat that answers your questions, the support form that listens — they are all composed from the same materials, by the same loom, in the same voice.

This is what Webspinner means by **Cognitive Content™**: a web that thinks.

## What You Meet When You Arrive

Visit iphloat.com and you find a website that pays attention. Not in a watching way — in a *listening* way. The pages you read compose themselves around what brought you to the site. The chat window in the corner is not a separate app; it is the same loom that wrote the rest of the page, opened so you can speak with it directly.

Ask whether iPhloat fits an iPhone 17 Pro Max in a kayak. Ask which colorway is most visible on the surface of green lake water. Ask what happens when the case takes a hard drop on a dock. The answer arrives woven from the site's own materials, in the site's own voice — *a page that can answer the question the author would have answered if they were in the room.*

That is the difference between a document and Cognitive Content. A document says only what it was written to say. Cognitive Content listens, explains, and remembers.

## How a Webspinner Site Behaves

Beyond what you see, a Webspinner site has rhythms you learn quickly.

- **The chat is brief.** Answers in the chat are concise. If you want more, you ask for more — the Weaver does not flood the panel with preamble.
- **The Cognitive Content panel shows one page at a time.** When the Weaver has more to say than belongs in chat, it composes a page and opens it in the panel beside the chat. One page, for the question you are on. The panel replaces; it does not pile up.
- **The site shows its work.** When the Weaver is fetching, considering, or composing, you see it happen in real time. Webspinner sites do not go silent. There are no spinning wheels with nothing behind them.
- **Light and dark, at your command.** Every Webspinner site offers a light theme and a dark theme, and you can switch between them whenever you like. The change is immediate, not at the next page load.

These are not features of iPhloat in particular. They are the rhythms of every Webspinner site, set here so that tenants two through one-hundred and beyond inherit them.

## The Three Things a Webspinner Site Is Born From

Every Webspinner site comes into the world from three offerings:

- **Imagery** — the photographs, illustrations, and brand assets that carry the visual voice of the place.
- **Prose** — a document the site's author writes with care. The substance of the brand. The tone. The things a patron must know.
- **Intent** — a single sentence that says, plainly, what this site is for.

That is what Webspinner needs to begin. Not a content management system. Not a sitemap. Three offerings, given with care.

The imagery, in particular, does more than illustrate. Its colors, palettes, and rhythms become part of the site's visual fabric — the design honors what the author offered rather than overruling it with a generic theme.

## How the Weaver Learns the Site

Before a Webspinner site opens its doors, it is *trained.* You may have heard this called **Train Your Dragon** — the metaphor is deliberate. The site's author takes the role of **Dragon Tamer**, and the offerings become **Dragon Biscuits**: imagery, prose, intent, and anything else the author wishes the loom to know. The site learns to speak in its own voice before the gate opens for visitors.

Training is not a one-time event. As an author offers new biscuits over time — a new product photo, a revised page of prose, a freshly written FAQ — the site is fed again, and its voice deepens accordingly.

This is one half of what Webspinner means by *AI Native — not AI assisted, not AI augmented. AI Native.* The other half is what happens at the moment the patron arrives.

## Where the Loom Lives

A Webspinner site's loom can live in one of three places. Each is a different posture toward where your words travel when you speak with the site.

- **Open Loom.** *The great loom of the world sharpens the threads.* The site reaches out to a distant loom for its words. Powerful. Familiar. Suitable for sites whose conversation is fully public.
- **Quiet Loom.** *Webspinner's own loom weaves every thread.* The site speaks through a loom that lives inside Webspinner.Cloud. Your words never leave the Foundation's care. This is the default loom for Webspinner sites, and the loom that serves iPhloat.
- **Hand Loom.** *Your own loom, your own thread.* For patrons who want their conversation to live entirely on their own device. Nothing leaves your machine. The site is still woven; the weaving simply happens in your hand.

Choosing which loom serves a site is a decision the site's author makes with the Weaver. Choosing which loom serves you, when you visit, is a decision the *site* respects on your behalf.

## The Weaver and Hagrid

You will meet two presences when you visit a Webspinner site, even if you never see them named.

The **Weaver** is the servant-leader of the site. Whether you are reading a page, asking the chat a question, or filling in a form, you are speaking with the Weaver. The Weaver composes each page from what the author has entrusted it to know — and *the Weaver remembers,* Webspinner's promise to patrons who have made themselves known.

**Hagrid** is the patient governor at the gate. He is the reason a Webspinner site does not embarrass itself or its patrons. Before any change reaches you — a new page, a new flow, a new product — Hagrid checks it warmly, clearly, and without apology. The discipline you do not see is the reason the experience you do see is worth having.

## What Stays Yours

Webspinner has named three privacy tiers a patron's conversation may belong to:

- **Just Me** — the conversation stays with the loom that served it.
- **My Fellows** — the conversation may be shared with patrons the patron has explicitly invited into a circle.
- **The Kingdom** — the conversation may contribute, with the patron's consent, to the wider Webspinner corpus.

The default is *Just Me.* Sharing is something a patron does by choice, never something a site does by reflex.

A Webspinner site is also never a hostage of the platform that birthed it. Any site can be **extracted** for its owner to host wherever they please, with no ongoing dependency on the Foundation's infrastructure. The author stays the protagonist.

## Why We Call It Build A Better Web

For thirty years the web has been documents — pages of text that cannot think, cannot adapt, cannot answer. Webspinner exists to end that era. Not by replacing the people who write, but by giving what they write a voice. The author stays the protagonist; the loom serves what the author meant.

iPhloat is the first public site woven on the Webspinner approach. A waterproof iPhone case that floats face-up — a small, exact promise. The site that sells it is held to the same standard.

This is what we mean when we say **Build A Better Web.**
