How iPhloat works

Three simple ideas, executed precisely.

The sealed buoyancy chamber

iPhloat’s outer frame is not solid. It is a single piece of precision-molded polycarbonate with a continuous, sealed micro-chamber running through the four corners and along the bottom edge. That chamber holds approximately 14 cubic centimeters of dry, low-density volume — enough to give the entire case-plus-phone assembly positive buoyancy of roughly 18 grams.

In practical terms: the case-plus-phone weighs about 230 grams, and it floats with approximately one-eighth of its volume above water. Because the chamber is distributed around the perimeter and weighted toward the back, the case orients itself screen-up within about two seconds of hitting the water. We tested this in fresh water, salt water, and chlorinated pool water at temperatures from 38°F to 95°F. The behavior is consistent. The screen finds the sky.

The double seal

The chamber is one of two sealed systems in the case. The other is the phone cavity itself. iPhloat uses a perimeter silicone gasket with a secondary lip seal at the charge port, the speaker grilles, and the camera deck. The two-piece shell closes with eight integrated snap-lock points and a bottom retention latch that audibly clicks when the seal is correctly seated.

The case is rated IP68 to 10 meters of submersion for 30 minutes. Unlike most premium cases, our seal does not have to keep your phone from sinking. It has to keep your phone dry while it floats. That is a meaningfully easier job, and we engineer to it.

Engineered to feel like an iPhone

We reject the assumption that ruggedness has to come at the cost of experience. iPhloat is MagSafe-compatible — the magnet array is calibrated to align cleanly through the case back. The 9H tempered-glass screen protector is laminated to the front shell with a clear optical layer; touch sensitivity is not detectably different from a naked phone. The buttons are individually molded with tactile click domes underneath, not flat membranes. The speakers sit behind ported acoustic channels that preserve frequency response in the voice range. The back panel is true clear, not frosted, so the iPhone’s own design — Apple logo, camera deck, color — shows through.

You do not stop seeing your phone when you put on iPhloat.
