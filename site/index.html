Every other premium iPhone case is engineered as if water is something to be kept out — and most of them do that part well. None of them is engineered as if water is something you might fall into. iPhloat is. We started by asking the question the rest of the category will not: what happens after the case fails? The honest answer, in every other product, is that your $1,200 phone goes to the bottom. iPhloat is built so that doesn’t happen — and because we know how it’s built, we’ll insure your phone against it.

Two ways to lose a phone on the water.

The waterproof phone case market today divides into two camps that each solve half the problem. On one side: premium hard cases that are sealed, drop-rated, and beautifully made — and denser than water. The day you drop one off a dock or a paddleboard, you watch a small dark rectangle disappear at about three feet per second. On the other side: floating pouches that float, but mute the speakers, fog the lens, blunt the buttons, and turn a $1,200 device into something you operate through a layer of vinyl.

iPhloat closes the gap. A precision-machined polycarbonate frame with an integrated 9H tempered-glass screen protector, tactile buttons, true-sound acoustics, and MagSafe compatibility — that floats face-up because of a sealed micro-buoyancy chamber molded into the frame itself. It floats the way a life jacket floats: passively, permanently, and with the screen facing the sky so you can see your phone before the current takes it.
