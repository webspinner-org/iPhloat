iPhloat Insurance

We replace your phone if our case fails. $5.99 a year.

When you pre-order iPhloat, you can add iPhloat Insurance for a single one-time charge of $5.99. That charge buys you twelve consecutive months of coverage, beginning the day your iPhloat ships and your case is fitted to your phone. The terms are simple, and they are written in plain English instead of legalese: if your iPhloat ever sinks, leaks, or otherwise fails to do what we said it would do — and your iPhone is damaged as a result — we replace your phone. Not a partial credit. Not a refurb voucher. The cost of a comparable new iPhone, remitted to you within fourteen days of a verified claim.

We are able to offer this because we are confident in the product. The buoyancy chamber is sealed at the molding stage and pressure-tested at the assembly stage. Each individual case carries a serial number that ties back to its production batch and its specific seal-test reading. If a case in field use ever fails, we know which batch it came from and we can recall any related units before another customer is affected. The insurance is not a marketing flourish; it is a feedback loop that makes the product better.

What it covers

Water damage to your iPhone caused by a sealed-failure or buoyancy-failure of your iPhloat case while the case is correctly fitted, latched, and within published depth and time limits (IP68, 10m, 30 minutes). Coverage applies in fresh water, salt water, and chlorinated pool water.

What it doesn’t cover

Damage caused by deliberate misuse, by exceeding the published depth or time limit, by use with a case that has not passed the user-runnable seal test (a 60-second pre-use check we describe in the documentation), or by physical damage to the phone from a drop unrelated to water exposure. The insurance is for the thing we promised — your phone, dry — not a general phone-damage policy.

Renewal

Year one is $5.99 and ends. We will publish renewal pricing for year two and beyond at least sixty days before your coverage expires; our intent is to keep renewals at or near the same price for the life of the product. There is no auto-renewal without your explicit opt-in.

Final policy terms will be drafted and reviewed by counsel prior to the Spring 2027 launch. The legal page will carry the binding agreement; the marketing prose above is governed by it.
