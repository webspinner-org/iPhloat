# iPhloat — product and site intent

## The product

iPhloat is a fully waterproof iPhone case engineered to float
face-up when dropped in water. It is built for the moments when
phones disappear — over the side of a boat, off a dock, into a
pool, into surf. iPhloat keeps the device dry, protected, and
visible on the surface so it can be retrieved.

The product is for boaters, anglers, paddlers, surfers,
kayakers, pool-side parents, dock hands, and anyone who has ever
watched their phone disappear beneath the surface.

## The site

The iPhloat brand site demonstrates an AI-Native approach to
commerce. Every patron-facing surface — the marketing pages,
the product Q&A, the customer support chat, the cart, the
checkout — is built on the Webspinner ecosystem.

What this means in practice:

- **Two-panel patron experience.** A chat panel for
  conversation; a Cognitive Content panel for what the site is
  showing in response. Brief on the chat side; rich on the
  content side.
- **Single-page Cognitive Content.** The right panel composes
  one page at a time for the patron's current question,
  replacing rather than stacking. Patrons see what they asked
  for; nothing else competes for attention.
- **Live progress, no silent waits.** The site always tells the
  patron what it is doing. No hourglass spinning behind a wall
  of silence.
- **Light and dark themes, switchable instantly.** Patron
  comfort matters. The toggle is visible; the change is
  real-time.
- **Imagery-derived design.** The colors, palette, type
  pairings, and layout density of the site come from the
  imagery the brand provided — not from a generic template.
- **Patron voice, always.** The site speaks in the brand's
  voice. Implementation details, vendor names, and
  infrastructure language never reach the patron.

## How iPhloat fits the Webspinner movement

iPhloat is a tenant in the Webspinner ecosystem — the first of
many public sites the ecosystem will host. The Webspinner
Foundation publishes the architecture, governance model, and
open-source security posture that every tenant inherits. See
the Foundation's public briefings for the deeper story of
*Cognitive Content*, *Webspinner Retrieval-Augmented Grounding*,
and the cell-based security model.

The promise to a brand owner is straightforward: the patron
experience belongs to the brand; the technology that delivers
it is owned, audited, and governed in the open.

## Pre-launch posture

iPhloat is in pre-launch. Patron-facing surfaces ship as each
clears its quality gates end-to-end. Until then, only invited
testers reach the live site. The brand voice, the product, and
the site are still being rehearsed.
